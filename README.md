Project README

Overview:
This project explores the behavior of reflections on a metallic surface, such as silver or steel. The goal is to mimic real-life reflection fading, where the reflected image becomes less distinct with distance, angle, or surface imperfections.

Key Concepts:
- Surface material: Silver and steel have high reflectivity and produce clear but not perfect reflections.
- Reflection fading: Real reflections lose contrast and detail due to scattering, surface roughness, and viewing angle.
- Realism: The project aims to balance sharpness and blur so the reflection appears natural rather than artificial.

Features:
- Simulated metallic surface with adjustable reflectivity.
- Gradual fade of reflection intensity based on distance and angle.
- Support for subtle blur or noise to represent surface texture.
- Focus on visual realism rather than perfect mirror behavior.

Usage:
- Set the surface material to silver or steel.
- Adjust parameters for reflection strength and fade distance.
- Fine-tune blur intensity to simulate surface imperfections.
- Test reflections under different lighting and viewing angles.

Notes:
- Silver typically produces brighter reflections with cooler highlights.
- Steel reflections are slightly darker and may include more diffuse scattering.
- The fade effect should be smooth and gradual to mimic natural reflection decay.

This README provides a foundation for understanding the project and how to configure the reflection simulation for a more lifelike result.
